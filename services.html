What We Offer

Interventional Pain Services

From precision-guided injections to advanced neuromodulation, our full spectrum of interventional procedures is designed to address pain at its source — not just mask the symptoms.

Spinal & Back Pain

Targeted therapies for herniated discs, stenosis, facet arthropathy, and degenerative spine conditions.

Epidural Steroid Injections

Fluoroscopy-guided injections deliver anti-inflammatory medication directly to the epidural space, providing relief for radiculopathy, disc herniation, and spinal stenosis.

Schedule Consultation →

Facet Joint Injections

Diagnostic and therapeutic injections targeting the small joints along the spine responsible for axial neck and back pain.

Schedule Consultation →

Medial Branch Blocks & RFA

Medial branch nerve blocks confirm facet-mediated pain; radiofrequency ablation then provides durable relief lasting 12–24 months in most patients.

Schedule Consultation →

Intradiscal Procedures

Minimally invasive treatments targeting the intervertebral disc itself, including intradiscal steroid injections and biologic therapies.

Schedule Consultation →

Nerve Pain & Neuropathy

Precision nerve-targeting therapies for sciatica, CRPS, peripheral neuropathy, and neuralgia syndromes.

Peripheral Nerve Blocks

Ultrasound-guided blocks targeting specific peripheral nerves to provide diagnostic clarity and immediate pain relief.

Schedule Consultation →

Sympathetic Nerve Blocks

Stellate ganglion, celiac plexus, and lumbar sympathetic blocks for CRPS, visceral pain, and vascular conditions.

Schedule Consultation →

Spinal Cord Stimulation

A reversible neuromodulation therapy that interrupts pain signals before they reach the brain — highly effective for CRPS, failed back surgery syndrome, and refractory neuropathy.

Schedule Consultation →

Ketamine Infusion Therapy

Low-dose IV ketamine protocols for CRPS, fibromyalgia, and refractory neuropathic pain conditions that have not responded to conventional treatments.

Schedule Consultation →

Joint & Musculoskeletal Pain

Injections and regenerative therapies for hip, knee, shoulder, and sacroiliac joint conditions.

Joint Corticosteroid Injections

Image-guided steroid injections into major joints provide rapid anti-inflammatory relief for arthritis, bursitis, and synovitis.

Schedule Consultation →

Sacroiliac Joint Injections

Fluoroscopy-guided SI joint injections for sacroiliitis and sacroiliac dysfunction — a frequently overlooked source of low back and buttock pain.

Schedule Consultation →

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP)

Regenerative injections using your own concentrated platelets to promote healing in tendons, ligaments, and arthritic joints.

Schedule Consultation →

Trigger Point Injections

Direct injection of local anesthetic and/or steroid into myofascial trigger points for immediate muscle pain relief and improved function.

Schedule Consultation →

Headache & Migraine

Interventional approaches to chronic migraine, occipital neuralgia, and cervicogenic headache syndromes.

Occipital Nerve Blocks

Targeted injections at the greater and lesser occipital nerves for occipital neuralgia and migraine prevention.

Schedule Consultation →

Botulinum Toxin (Botox) for Migraine

FDA-approved BOTOX injections for chronic migraine — up to 31 injection sites targeting head and neck muscles every 12 weeks.

Schedule Consultation →

Sphenopalatine Ganglion Block

A minimally invasive intranasal procedure providing rapid relief for cluster headaches and acute migraine episodes.

Schedule Consultation →

Post-Surgical & Cancer Pain

Specialized care for patients with persistent post-operative pain and cancer-related pain syndromes.

Failed Back Surgery Syndrome

Comprehensive evaluation and treatment for persistent or recurrent pain following lumbar or cervical spine surgery, including spinal cord stimulation.

Schedule Consultation →

Cancer Pain Management

Oncology-focused pain protocols including intrathecal drug delivery, neurolytic nerve blocks, and palliative interventional procedures.

Schedule Consultation →

Intrathecal Drug Delivery

Implantable pump systems deliver pain medication directly into the spinal fluid at doses far lower than oral therapy — maximizing effect while minimizing side effects.

Schedule Consultation →
