What We Offer

Our Services & Procedures

We offer a comprehensive range of evidence-based interventional treatments — from diagnostic nerve blocks to advanced neuromodulation — all performed by a fellowship-trained specialist using image-guided precision.

Procedures We Perform

Epidural Steroid Injections

Spine · Radiculopathy

Epidural steroid injections (ESIs) deliver powerful anti-inflammatory medication directly into the epidural space of the spine, targeting the source of nerve root irritation. Under live fluoroscopic guidance, we access cervical, thoracic, or lumbar epidural spaces via interlaminar or transforaminal approaches for maximum precision.

ESIs are highly effective for disc herniations, spinal stenosis, degenerative disc disease, and radiculopathy. Most patients experience significant relief within 3–7 days that can last for months, often allowing them to engage productively in physical therapy.

  • Cervical, thoracic & lumbar approaches
  • Interlaminar and transforaminal techniques
  • Image-guided under fluoroscopy
  • Outpatient procedure, 20–30 minutes

Nerve Blocks

Diagnostic · Therapeutic

Nerve blocks involve injecting local anesthetic — sometimes with steroid — around a specific nerve or nerve plexus to interrupt pain signals. They serve both diagnostic purposes (confirming a pain source) and therapeutic purposes (providing lasting relief).

We perform a wide spectrum of nerve block procedures including medial branch blocks, occipital nerve blocks, stellate ganglion blocks, celiac plexus blocks, and peripheral nerve blocks under ultrasound or fluoroscopic guidance.

  • Medial branch & facet joint blocks
  • Occipital nerve blocks for headache
  • Sympathetic nerve blocks (stellate, celiac, lumbar)
  • Peripheral nerve blocks (knee, shoulder, hip)

Radiofrequency Ablation

Long-Term Relief · Spine

Radiofrequency ablation (RFA) uses precisely controlled heat energy to interrupt the pain signals carried by specific nerves, offering relief that typically lasts 9–18 months or longer. It is the gold standard for chronic facet-mediated back and neck pain confirmed by positive diagnostic nerve blocks.

We offer both conventional RFA and cooled RFA (for sacroiliac joint pain), as well as pulsed RFA for sensitive nerves where thermal ablation is not appropriate. The outpatient procedure takes approximately 45–60 minutes.

  • Cervical, thoracic & lumbar medial branch RFA
  • Cooled RFA for sacroiliac joint pain
  • Pulsed RFA for peripheral neuropathy
  • Relief lasting 9–18+ months in most patients

Spinal Cord Stimulation

Neuromodulation · Chronic Pain

Spinal cord stimulation (SCS) is an advanced neuromodulation therapy that uses mild electrical impulses to modulate pain signals before they reach the brain. A small implantable device delivers these signals via leads placed in the epidural space, offering transformative relief for patients who have not responded to other treatments.

We work with the leading SCS systems (Medtronic, Abbott, Boston Scientific) and offer high-frequency (10 kHz), burst, and traditional stimulation modes. Every patient undergoes a trial period before permanent implantation to confirm effectiveness.

  • Failed back surgery syndrome
  • Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS)
  • Peripheral neuropathy & diabetic neuropathy
  • Multi-vendor trial available before implant

Ketamine Infusions

CRPS · Refractory Pain

Low-dose intravenous ketamine infusions are a breakthrough option for patients with treatment-resistant chronic pain, including complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS), fibromyalgia, neuropathic pain, and refractory pain syndromes. Ketamine acts as an NMDA receptor antagonist, resetting sensitized pain pathways in the central nervous system.

Infusions are administered in our monitored, comfortable infusion suite. A typical course is a series of infusions over several days, with many patients experiencing significant relief lasting weeks to months. Booster infusions can extend these results.

  • CRPS / RSD — primary indication
  • Fibromyalgia & central sensitization
  • Refractory neuropathic pain
  • Monitored infusion suite, individualized dosing

PRP & Regenerative Therapies

Joints · Tendons · Ligaments

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy harnesses your body's own healing factors to accelerate tissue repair in joints, tendons, and ligaments. Blood is drawn, centrifuged to concentrate platelets and growth factors, and injected precisely into the affected area under ultrasound guidance.

PRP is an excellent option for knee osteoarthritis, shoulder rotator cuff pathology, hip labral issues, and chronic tendinopathies where conventional treatments have provided limited relief. It offers a non-surgical pathway to healing with minimal downtime.

  • Knee & hip osteoarthritis
  • Rotator cuff & shoulder tendinopathy
  • Chronic tendinitis & ligament injury
  • Ultrasound-guided precision injection

Joint Injections & Viscosupplementation

Knee · Hip · Shoulder · SI Joint

Targeted joint injections deliver corticosteroids or hyaluronic acid (viscosupplementation) directly into arthritic or inflamed joints for meaningful, lasting relief. All joint injections are performed under ultrasound guidance for confirmed needle placement and optimal outcomes.

We treat knee, hip, shoulder, ankle, and sacroiliac joints. Viscosupplementation (Hyalgan, Synvisc) is especially effective for knee osteoarthritis, restoring the natural lubrication that arthritis depletes over time.

  • Corticosteroid joint injections
  • Hyaluronic acid (viscosupplementation)
  • Sacroiliac joint injections
  • All major joints, ultrasound-guided
