Dr. Marcus J. Thornton, MD

Founder & Medical Director — Interventional Pain Specialist

Background & Training

Dr. Marcus Thornton grew up in a mid-sized coastal community where access to specialized medical care often meant long drives or delayed diagnoses. Witnessing neighbors manage chronic conditions without proper support shaped his conviction early on: exceptional pain care should not be a privilege reserved for those who can travel far to receive it.

After earning his medical degree from Grandview University School of Medicine, Dr. Thornton completed his residency in Physical Medicine & Rehabilitation at Harborview Regional Medical Center. He then pursued a competitive fellowship in Interventional Pain Medicine at the Westbrook Medical Institute, where he trained under nationally recognized specialists in neuromodulation and image-guided spine procedures.

He established Apex Spine & Pain Center in 2008 with a clear mission: to bring fellowship-level interventional techniques directly to patients in the community — eliminating the need for lengthy referral chains or out-of-region travel.

Clinical Focus

Dr. Thornton specializes in fluoroscopy- and ultrasound-guided procedures for spinal, joint, and neuropathic pain conditions. He has particular expertise in spinal cord stimulation, radiofrequency ablation of facet and sacroiliac joints, and comprehensive management of complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS). He is among a select group of practitioners in the region trained in high-frequency SCS and dorsal root ganglion (DRG) stimulation techniques.

Beyond procedures, Dr. Thornton takes a multimodal approach to every patient — integrating pharmacologic management, physical rehabilitation coordination, and psychological support to address pain's full impact on daily life and function.

Our Approach

Three Principles That Guide Every Visit

01

Listen First

Every new patient receives a minimum 60-minute consultation. We review your full history, imaging, and prior treatments before recommending anything. The right procedure for the wrong diagnosis helps no one.

02

Precision Over Volume

We perform procedures with fluoroscopic or ultrasound guidance — every time. Image guidance isn't optional at Apex; it's the standard. Accuracy determines outcomes, and outcomes determine whether you come back.

03

Partnership, Not Prescription

You leave every appointment understanding exactly what was done, why, and what to expect. Your treatment plan evolves with your progress. We measure success the same way you do — by what you're able to do again.
